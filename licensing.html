Licensing

What Licensing Means

Licensing grants permission to use the film internally as a recurring resource.

  • Showing it to multiple groups
  • Returning to it over time
  • Building facilitated experiences around it

There are no subscriptions.
No portals.
No usage tracking systems.

This mirrors how schools and organizations already operate.

How Licensing Is Enforced

  • Clear license language
  • Clear invoice descriptions
  • Institutional good faith

No DRM. No policing.

Rights & Where You Can Show the Film

These licenses grant internal permission to use the film and materials in closed settings — classrooms, meetings, retreats, staff trainings, advisory periods, and other internal reflection spaces.

Public performance, public online streaming, or public social media distribution is not included unless a separate agreement is made.

What You Receive After Licensing

  • Private access link to the licensed film
  • Facilitator Guide (PDF)
  • Participant Discussion Materials (PDF)
  • Optional alignment and context notes (PDF)

These materials are yours to download and use internally under your license tier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we show this publicly?
This licensing is for internal use. Public performance or streaming requires a separate arrangement.

Can we upgrade later?
Yes — you can upgrade your license at any time.

Can different teams within our organization use it?
Yes — use depends on your selected tier, and upgrades can expand access.

Upgrades & Renewals

You may upgrade your license to a higher tier at any time. Standard licenses may be renewed beyond the original term upon request. Licenses are organization-wide and may be transferred within the licensed entity.
